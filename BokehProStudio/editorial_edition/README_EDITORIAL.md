# 📰 Bokeh Studio Pro - Editorial Edition

## What This Is

The **Editorial Edition** simulates realistic depth of field by working with **depth planes** rather than radial distance. Click on your subject, and everything at that depth stays sharp while the background blurs naturally.

Perfect for sports, equestrian, event photography, and any work requiring editorial/photojournalistic standards.

---

## How It Works

1. Open `bokeh_studio_editorial.html` in any web browser
2. Upload your photo
3. Click **anywhere** on your subject (horse, rider, player, equipment)
4. **Entire subject at that depth stays sharp**
5. Background at different depth blurs naturally
6. Download your enhanced photo

---

## The Key Difference

**Creative Edition:** Click a point → blur radiates from that point  
**Editorial Edition:** Click a point → entire depth plane stays sharp  

### Example: Equestrian Jump Shot

**Creative:** Click on horse's head → head sharp, horse's rear blurred  
**Editorial:** Click on horse → entire horse + rider + jump bars sharp, arena blurred  

**Editorial respects scene geometry. Creative gives artistic control.**

## Pro Tips

### For Equestrian Photography:
1. **Click anywhere on horse or rider** (depth plane, not point)
2. Use **85mm f/1.4** or **Sony GM** preset
3. Depth plane tolerance: **12-15%** (narrower sharp zone)
4. Background blur: **20-30**
5. **Entire horse + rider + bars stay sharp**, arena blurs

### For Sports Photography:
1. Click on player
2. Use **50mm f/1.8** preset (more natural)
3. Depth plane tolerance: **15-18%**
4. Lower blur (**15-25**) to keep context visible
5. Player sharp, crowd/background naturally blurred

### For Event Photography:
1. Click on main subject
2. Use **50mm f/1.8** preset
3. Subtle blur (**10-18**) for natural look
4. Tolerance: **18-20%** (wider acceptance)
5. Clean background without losing context

## Controls Explained

### Depth Plane Tolerance
**What it does:** Controls how much depth variation is considered "in focus"

- **5-10%:** Narrow zone (like f/1.2 - very selective)
- **12-15%:** Standard (like f/2.8)
- **18-25%:** Wide zone (like f/4 - more forgiving)
- **25-30%:** Very wide (like f/5.6)

**For equestrian:** Keep it 12-15% to blur arena while keeping horse/rider sharp

### Blur Falloff
**Soft:** Gradual transition (dreamy look)  
**Medium:** Balanced (most natural)  
**Sharp:** Quick transition (dramatic)  

### Background Blur
**10-15:** Subtle cleanup  
**20-30:** Standard depth of field  
**30-40:** Aggressive separation

## How Depth Mapping Works

The tool automatically analyzes your image to estimate depth:

- **Vertical position** (top = usually background)
- **Edge sharpness** (sharper edges = closer)
- **Saturation** (more saturated = often foreground)

When you click on your subject, it finds all pixels at similar depth and keeps them sharp.

**Note:** Works best when subject is roughly mid-frame and background is clearly behind them.

## When to Use Editorial vs Creative

**Use Editorial Edition when:**
- Entire subject needs to stay sharp
- Sports, equestrian, or action photography
- Editorial/photojournalistic standards required
- You want realistic lens simulation
- Subject has depth (like horse + rider)

**Use Creative Edition when:**
- Portrait/headshot work (just face sharp)
- Artistic control desired
- Point-based blur is what you want
- Social media / artistic work
