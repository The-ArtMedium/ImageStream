## What Is This?

A professional-grade tool that simulates shallow depth of field effects (bokeh) without requiring expensive fast lenses. Created to democratize creative tools that companies charge $400-$600/year for.

## Bokeh Style Guide

### What Different Bokeh Looks Like

#### **Circular Bokeh** ⭕
- **Characteristics**: Smooth, round out-of-focus highlights
- **Real-world examples**: Modern Canon RF, Sony GM, Nikon Z lenses
- **Best for**: Clean, professional portraits; minimalist aesthetics
- **Notes**: Most versatile and "neutral" looking

#### **Hexagonal Bokeh** ⬡
- **Characteristics**: Six-sided bokeh balls, slightly harder edges
- **Real-world examples**: Vintage lenses, budget primes (6-blade apertures)
- **Best for**: Nostalgic feel, character-driven work, artistic shots
- **Notes**: Can add visual interest but may be distracting in some contexts

#### **Octagonal Bokeh** ⯃
- **Characteristics**: Eight-sided bokeh, smoother than hexagonal
- **Real-world examples**: High-end primes (Sigma Art, Zeiss)
- **Best for**: Professional work needing smooth but defined bokeh
- **Notes**: Good middle ground between perfectly circular and geometric

#### **Sony Feathered Bokeh** 🌊
- **Characteristics**: Painterly smoothness, subtle render lines, ultra-creamy transitions
- **Real-world examples**: Sony 85mm f/1.4 GM, 100mm f/2.8 STF GM
- **Best for**: High-end portrait work, subjects needing maximum separation
- **Notes**: This is the "holy grail" bokeh - costs $1,800+ in real glass
- **Why it's special**: The STF (Smooth Trans Focus) has an apodization element that creates impossibly smooth transitions

## How To Use

### Quick Start
1. **Open** `pro_bokeh_studio.html` in any modern browser
2. **Upload** your photo (drag & drop or click)
3. **Click** on your subject to set the focus point
4. **Choose** a lens preset or customize manually
5. **Adjust** blur strength and focus range to taste
6. **Download** your enhanced photo

### Pro Tips

#### For Equestrian Photography
- Set focus point on the rider's face or horse's eye
- Use **85mm f/1.4** preset for natural subject isolation
- Adjust **Focus Range** to keep horse and rider sharp while blurring background
- Enable **Vignette** (20-30%) to draw eye to subject
- Use **Highlight Boost** to make arena lights/sun flare beautifully

#### For Portraits
- Try **Sony Feathered** for ultra-professional look
- Keep **Edge Softness** at 40-60% for natural transitions
- Use **Highlight Boost** on backlit subjects for dreamy glow
- **Focus Range** of 100-200 works well for headshots

#### For Events
- **50mm f/1.8** preset gives natural, versatile look
- Lower **Blur Intensity** (10-15) for subtle enhancement
- Disable **Highlight Boost** if background has many light sources
- Use comparison slider to ensure it doesn't look over-processed

### Understanding the Controls

**Blur Intensity** (0-50)
- 0-10: Subtle separation, natural look
- 10-25: Standard shallow DOF (f/1.8-f/2.8 range)
- 25-40: Aggressive blur (f/1.2-f/1.4 range)  
- 40-50: Extreme dreamy effect

**Focus Range** (30-600)
- 30-100: Tight focus, rapid blur falloff (macro/close-up)
- 100-250: Portrait range (most common)
- 250-400: Environmental portraits (more context)
- 400-600: Subtle depth, gentle separation

**Highlight Strength** (0-100)
- 0-25: Subtle enhancement
- 25-50: Noticeable bokeh balls on bright spots
- 50-75: Pronounced glow (Sony GM territory)
- 75-100: Dreamy, ethereal (STF lens simulation)
